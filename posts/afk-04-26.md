### Science and Tech and Game Theory

Michael Pollan has a new book on consciousness (after psychedelics someone got really into existential philosophy and brain stuff, so special), and listening to his interview on 'A Slight Change of Plans', he ALSO went down a rabbit hole on [the 'plant neuroscience' community](https://blog.georeactor.com/plantbio-1).

Cloudflare continues to lead in technical blog posts about post-quantum encryption / cryptography (PQE or PQC). They plan to be [fully ready by 2029](https://blog.cloudflare.com/post-quantum-roadmap/). <br/>
Google's latest research also got to the bottom of rumors [about cryptocurrency](https://research.google/blog/safeguarding-cryptocurrency-by-disclosing-quantum-vulnerabilities-responsibly/). <br/>
And I haven't seen Meta blogging about this before, but they have [a roadmap / case study](https://engineering.fb.com/2026/04/16/security/post-quantum-cryptography-migration-at-meta-framework-lessons-and-takeaways/) written up.

[SweepTheStrait.com](https://sweepthestrait.com) is a Hormuz-shaped Minesweeper game. I spent hours on this recently.  First I appreciate a detail of Minesweeper, which is if there's a 50-50 pick of a mine or a safe space, at some point you have to click and it cannot get any easier or safer if you spend time somewhere else on the puzzle.<br/>
Also I had not thought about the difficulty of an irregular-shaped Minesweeper before. Consider a 1D linear Minesweeper, where you know to skip a cell that you know is a mine, but you have no info if the next cell down the line is a '1', '2', or a mine. If you're going to play this game 100s of times, you should start with the line in the northwest each time to avoid wasting time on normal sections.

If the top proprietary AIs are working with the military, they use the models before real attacks, and the military can push the industry around if they hit safeguards… is AI safety already over? What were all of the lectures and books and Effective Altruism hot takes supposed to prevent? (Side note: I wonder if EA discourse, including the "Claude Constitution", led to some of the rhetoric around the US Constitution during the Anthropic-Pentagon controversy) <br/>
Then I was surprised to see the Middlebury Institute post [a lecture on Gemini doing war games](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LXDFw4IhM8w). I can understand why the military might test this to harvest new ideas from this or future LLMs, but I am puzzled about the claim of 750 simulations, 1.3 million words, etc. I would think after 10 or 100 simulations, the marginal value can't be that high. Either your superintelligent LLM can meaningfully predict archetypal nuclear war timelines, or it is writing randomly, or we're saying that nuclear war is so unpredictable that 100 different scenarios don't cover it.

The movie follows Robert Graysmith's books from 1986 and 2002, ending with multiple people believing that Arthur Leigh Allen was the killer. Also in 2002, a DNA sample failed to match Allen. That's mentioned in the movie epilogue, but I think most people remember Allen. <br/>
True believers say that this sample was from the face of the stamp, so it would be contaminated. Considering how forensic genealogy and trace DNA have improved since, including the arrest of EAR/ONS in 2018, I'm surprised that there haven't been announcements. Or at least an expectation that families provide a DNA sample before they do a Zodiac suspect press conference.
